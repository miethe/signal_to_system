AI coding tools work at the individual task level. The velocity gains are real — and repeatable — but they stop compounding the moment code leaves one developer's machine. Review queues stretch, context collapses between sessions, accountability dissolves, and Shadow AI opens security gaps nobody sees until they cost six figures.

This series is the operator's answer. Five posts, each targeting one layer of the missing infrastructure:

1. **The AI Productivity Paradox** — why task-level gains don't translate to team-level delivery, and the four structural failure modes every org eventually hits.
2. **Intent-Driven Development** — writing specs that agents can execute deterministically, and what it means when the developer's role shifts from syntax to intent.
3. **The Control Plane** — the context, memory, and verification infrastructure that cures Agent Amnesia at the system level instead of the session level.
4. **Observability for Agentic Systems** — making token burn, regressions, and silent failures visible before they compound.
5. **Enterprise Adoption** — the operating model, governance tiers, and org shape required to scale any of this beyond one developer's workflow.

Written from inside the work: on teams I'm accountable for and projects I ship myself. Less framework branding, more of the specific patterns that have held up under real workloads.
