**Status**: Planning phase (Deep research notebook active)

**Audience**: CTOs, Technical Executives, System Architects, Platform Leaders; Secondary: Senior/Staff Engineers  
**Scope**: Foundational CS concepts reinterpreted for agentic era through AI-assisted dialogue

## Core Thesis

In the age of agentic AI, one of the highest-value uses of AI is not generating answers faster, but helping experienced engineers and architects re-enter foundational ideas in structured dialogue—so they can reinterpret those ideas for modern systems, workflows, and leadership.

**Overarching Story**: Engineering teams increasingly rely on "vibe coding" and prompt-driven generation, treating LLMs as oracles that bypass rigorous design. However, a different path is emerging. When experienced technical leaders use AI as an intellectual dialogue partner (not a summarizer), they can reconnect with the foundational canon and translate it into modern engineering leverage. This series demonstrates that pattern.

## Key Decision Points for Drafters

1. **Epistemology focus**: This series is about HOW technical leaders learn with AI, not just WHAT they learn. Emphasize method over content.
2. **Avoid nostalgia**: Don't position as "remember the good old days of CS canon." Frame as "these ideas are urgent now."
3. **Avoid AI mysticism**: AI is a tool for dialogue, not an oracle. Keep grounded in rigorous reading and thinking.
4. **Audience calibration**: Primary audience is CTOs/architects. Secondary is Staff Engineers. Keep technical depth but executive applicability.
5. **Link back to practice**: Every post should end with "What a CTO/Architect should do differently." Don't stay abstract.
6. **Source grounding**: All claims should trace back to the canonical sources. This is a scholarly exercise masquerading as a blog.
